Understanding the Threat

What Is AML Layering?

The second — and most dangerous — stage of money laundering. Criminals move illegal funds through cascading networks of accounts, shell companies, crypto wallets, and jurisdictions to destroy the audit trail.

01

Placement

Illegal money enters the financial system via cash deposits, smurfing, or structuring to avoid detection thresholds.

02
AI FOCUS

Layering

Money is moved through complex chains — multiple accounts, wire transfers, crypto conversions — to hide its origin.

03

Integration

Laundered money re-enters the economy appearing legitimate through real estate, businesses, or luxury investments.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is AML layering?
AML layering is the second stage of money laundering where criminals move illegal funds through complex chains of transactions — multiple bank accounts, shell companies, crypto wallets — to obscure the original source and frustrate investigators.
Can this platform detect real money laundering?
AML Shield AI is an AI-assisted risk analysis platform that identifies patterns statistically associated with money laundering. It helps compliance teams prioritize investigations. A human compliance expert must make the final legal determination.
Is this a certified legal compliance tool?
No. This platform provides AI-assisted risk analysis and does not replace certified AML compliance or legal review. Always consult certified AML officers and legal counsel for regulatory filings and legal decisions.
What does the Neural Network Visualizer show?
The Neural Network Visualizer shows a real-time animated representation of the AI processing pipeline — from input transaction data through 6 neural layers including Graph GNN, Behavioral Analysis, Pattern Matching, Risk Scoring, and final Output. It demonstrates how confidence builds as data flows through each decision layer.
What is the Behavioral Biometrics Timeline?
The Behavioral Timeline profiles each account's transaction behavior over 30 days. It identifies anomaly spikes, dormant-to-active patterns, velocity changes, and high-risk events. You can select any account and see exactly when and how suspicious behavior emerged.
Can it detect suspicious crypto transactions?
Yes. Dedicated crypto monitoring covers 50+ blockchains. Detects mixing, tumbling, chain-hopping, rapid exchange transfers, sanctioned wallet addresses, and DeFi-based layering strategies.
Does it generate AML compliance reports?
Yes. Auto-generates detailed SARs including case ID, risk level, suspicious pattern analysis, AI reasoning, accounts involved, transaction summary, and recommended compliance actions. Exportable as PDF.
What is the Risk Heatmap Analytics feature?
The Risk Heatmap shows suspicious transaction intensity across a 7-day × 24-hour grid, revealing exactly which time windows carry the highest AML risk. Switch between Risk Score, Transaction Volume, and Alert Count views. Hover any cell for detailed stats, click to view alerts from that window. AI generates actionable insights about peak risk periods.
What is the Entity Relationship Graph Explorer?
The Entity Graph is an interactive force-directed network visualization showing all accounts, crypto wallets, and shell companies with their money flow connections. Drag nodes to rearrange, click to inspect risk details, add new nodes, and flag suspicious entities. Red edges show high-risk money flows, animated packets show real-time transaction directions.
What is the AI Compliance Copilot?
The AI Compliance Copilot is an intelligent chat assistant trained on AML regulations, FATF guidelines, RBI compliance rules, and financial crime patterns. Ask it anything — from "what is smurfing?" to "how does the risk score work?" or "what triggers a SAR filing?" Click the purple robot icon at the bottom-left to open it anytime.
